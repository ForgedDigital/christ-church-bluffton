Our Liturgy

The Book of Common Prayer

Our worship follows the historic patterns found in the Book of Common Prayer, connecting us to Christians across centuries and continents.

The liturgy guides us through confession, Scripture readings, prayers, and the celebration of Holy Communion. This rhythm of worship shapes our hearts and minds toward God week after week.

Whether you're new to liturgical worship or have been practicing it your whole life, you'll find our services accessible and meaningful. We provide service bulletins to help everyone follow along and participate fully.

Sacred Music

Hymns & Worship

Music is an integral part of our worship. We sing hymns ancient and modern that teach sound doctrine and lift our hearts to God. Our musical tradition draws from the rich heritage of Anglican hymnody while remaining accessible to all.

“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly, teaching and admonishing one another in all wisdom, singing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, with thankfulness in your hearts to God.”

— Colossians 3:16 (ESV)
